About Ethiopia


Ethiopia is a country in North Eastern Africa.
It is known as "the land of origins" because of its important archaeological and paleontological discoveries.
The earliest human ancestors were found in the Afar Depression in northeastern Ethiopia

AXUM
Aksum is a city in northern Ethiopia.
It's known for its tall, carved obelisks,
relics of the ancient Kingdom of Aksum.
Most are in the northern Stelae Park, including a huge fallen pillar, now in pieces.

Lalibela
Lalibela is a town in the Amhara region of northern Ethiopia.
It's known for its distinctive rock-cut churches dating from the 12th
and 13th centuries, which are pilgrimage sites for Coptic Christians.

Walia
The Walia ibex is a vulnerable species of ibex.
It is sometimes considered an endemic subspecies of the Alpine ibex.
